$$ \{\gamma^\mu, \gamma^\nu\} = 2\eta^{\mu \nu}\times\mathbb{I}_{n\times n} $$
Dirac Algebra

Where $n$ is the dimension of the matrices and $\mathbb{I}_{n\times n}$ the corresponding identity matrix.

The matrices that satisfy these anticommutation relations are reffered to as gamma or Dirac matrices.

$$ \delta \phi := (-1)^{np + n + 1 + s} \ast d \ast \phi $$
Coderivative

Where $\phi$ is a $p$-form defined in a open subset of $\mathbb{R}^n$, which has an orientation $\omega$ and a a metric $g$ of signature $r-s$. $\ast$ is the Hodge star and $d$ is the exterior derivative.

Properties
$$ \delta^2 = 0$$
LaTeX
\delta \phi := (-1)^{np + n + 1 + s} \ast d \ast \phi
$$ \Delta:= -(d\delta + \delta d) $$
Laplacian (d'Alambertian)

This is a map from and to $p$-forms. It's called Laplacian if the metric is positive-definite. If $r = 1$, it's called the d'Alambert operator and written $\Box$.
