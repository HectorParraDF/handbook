Hodge star squared

Where $\omega$ is a $p$-form, $m$ is the dimension of the manifold $M$ and $\alpha$ is equal to zero if $M$ is Riemannian and equal to one if $M$ is Lorentzian.

Particular cases

For $M = (\mathbb{R}^4, \eta)$, with $\eta$ a Minkowskian metric,

$$ \ast \ast \omega = (-1)^{1 + p} \omega,$$

i.e., for forms of even $p$ such as the field strength $F$, $\ast^2 = -1$, while for those with odd $p$ such as the potential $A$ or the current $j$, $\ast^2 = 1$.
